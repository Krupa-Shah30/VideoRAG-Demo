Every input or output of a computer is effectively a type of information which can be represented [34.28s] by on or off electrical signals for ones and zeros. This circuit will only give you a one if the first signal and the second signal are both a one. An adder circuit takes in two individual bits, each one a one or a zero, and adds them together [145.92s] to calculate the sum. Different electrical circuits can perform other simple calculations, like subtraction [196.36s] or multiplication [197.96s] Today's computer circuits are microscopic in size and way, way faster.
